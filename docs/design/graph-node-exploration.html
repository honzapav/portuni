Portuni · Graph studies · Apr 2026

Reading a node
at a glance.

Today a leaf node carries one signal — the entity type, painted as a coloured disc. Lifecycle states whisper through tinted borders; owners stay invisible until you open the detail pane. Four directions for letting the graph answer two more questions without forcing you to click: what shape is this work in, and who carries it.

A · Aurora ring
A / 01

Aurora ring — state orbits the disc.

Keep the existing coloured disc untouched. Wrap it with a status ring whose shape, colour and motion answer "how is this work doing?" Owner clings to the lower right like a small moon — the same place every time.

State ring · decoder

activeoperating solid ring, healthy hue
in_progressimplementing arc with comet head, slow rotation
at_riskneeds_attention amber pulse
broken red, broken segments
on_hold grey, finely dashed
archivedretiredcancelled ghosted, disc dimmed

Owner · always at 4 o'clock

A 22-px disc with two-letter initials. Stroke colour echoes the entity type so the satellite still feels family. Empty owner = no satellite.

Strengths

  • Doesn't disturb the existing palette or layout physics.
  • Motion makes "in progress" and "at risk" pop at any zoom.
  • Owner placement is consistent — eye learns to hunt 4 o'clock.

Tradeoffs

  • Every node grows ~30 % to fit ring + satellite.
  • Animation budget grows with graph size — needs throttling.
  • Two-letter initials collide on common names (J. Pavel vs. J. Pav).
B · Initials core
B / 02

Initials core — the node is the owner.

Flip the hierarchy. The avatar is the centre of gravity; type and state become atmosphere around it. The graph reads like a roster — Jan owns these three things, Marie owns those two — and accountability becomes the dominant gestalt.

What encodes what

Halo gradient = entity type (project, process, area, …)
Initials disc = owner; — if unowned
Ring = lifecycle state, same vocabulary as direction A

Strengths

  • Ownership becomes the first thing the eye picks up.
  • Great for "who's overloaded?" or "who owns the broken stuff?"
  • Halo + ring still encode type and state, just demoted.

Tradeoffs

  • Nodes must be ≥ 44 px for legible 2-letter initials.
  • Loses the simple "field of coloured stars" feel of the current graph.
  • Unowned items look orphaned; can be a feature or a bug.
C · Status sigil
C / 03

Status sigil — annotated atom.

The disc keeps its size; two small chips dock at its corners. State sits top-right as an icon — check, spinner, warning, X, pause. Owner sits bottom-right as initials. Reads like a notification badge on an app icon.

State glyph · decoder

activeoperatingdone green check
in_progressimplementing blue spinner
at_riskneeds_attention amber triangle
broken red ×
on_hold grey pause

Strengths

  • Most legible state encoding — icons read at a glance.
  • Disc size unchanged; existing layout physics keep working.
  • Glyph vocabulary maps cleanly to status pills in detail pane.

Tradeoffs

  • Two chips per node = visually busier; risks "Christmas tree" look at scale.
  • Glyph chips can collide with edges in dense clusters.
  • Furthest from current aesthetic — most engineering work.
D · Constellation pip
D / 04

Constellation pip — semiotics over chrome.

Most restrained. The disc stays small; state is encoded as the disc's fill pattern, not as added orbiting elements. Owner becomes a small monospaced tag floating beneath the label — almost a caption.

Fill pattern · decoder

activeoperating solid fill
in_progressimplementing half-fill, like a moon phase
at_risk hatched overlay
broken red strike-through
on_holdbacklog hollow ring, dashed

Strengths

  • Cheapest to render — no animation, no extra geometry.
  • Survives extreme zoom-out: hatching and X-marks remain readable.
  • Owner caption stays optional and legible without crowding the disc.

Tradeoffs

  • Pattern vocabulary takes a beat to learn.
  • Owner tag adds a third line below each label — vertical sprawl.
  • Hatched at-risk can clash visually with adjacent process-yellow.

Same scenario, four readings.

One node, one truth: Vendor invoice approval — a process that's at risk, owned by MK. Each direction tells the same story differently.

Director's pick

Aurora ring,
then graduate to sigil.

Aurora (A) preserves what already works. The disc keeps its identity, the ring upgrades the existing border treatment instead of replacing it, and the owner satellite borrows from a familiar visual idiom — a moon orbiting a planet. It's the smallest leap from today, and motion does the heavy lifting on the two states that matter most operationally: in progress and at risk.

Once the ring is in place, the status sigil (C) becomes a free upgrade for power users — same disc, just a small chip toggled by a viewport setting. Keep direction B in your back pocket for an "ownership view" toggle that re-frames the same graph around people instead of work. D is the right answer if performance ever forces a retreat from animation.

One pragmatic note: introduce the new node grammar behind a feature flag and ship with a two-week period where the legend (the decoder above) is one click away from the canvas. Vocabulary is half the design.
